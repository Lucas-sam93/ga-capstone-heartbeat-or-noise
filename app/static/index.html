Important Notice

BeatCheck is a screening tool only. It does not diagnose heart conditions and is not a medical device. Always consult a qualified medical professional if you have concerns about your heart health.

Upload your data

Export your data from the Apple Health app and upload a CSV or XML file. BeatCheck analyses your heart rate data from the past 90 days.

How to export from Apple Health

1

Open the Health app on your iPhone. On the Summary tab, tap your profile photo or initials in the top-right corner to open your profile page.

2

Scroll to the very bottom of the profile page and tap Export All Health Data. A confirmation prompt will appear — tap Export. Apple will prepare the file; this can take 1–5 minutes if you have several years of data.

3

An iOS share sheet will appear. Tap Save to Files, choose a folder (e.g. iCloud Drive or On My iPhone), then tap Save. Apple saves a file called export.zip. Open the Files app, find that ZIP, and tap-and-hold it to select Uncompress. Open the resulting folder and locate export.xml — upload that file here.

How BeatCheck works
8,187 clinical ECG recordings
84% sensitivity
0.91 AUROC

BeatCheck was trained on the Physionet 2017 AF Classification Challenge — a clinical dataset used globally for cardiac rhythm research. It extracts 8 heart rate variability features from your data and uses a Support Vector Machine to score each 30-minute window of activity against patterns seen in clinical ECG recordings.

Validated on 35 subjects with simultaneous wearable PPG and clinical ECG labels, the model retains discriminative ability across sensing modalities (AUROC 0.86). This is a research prototype — not a certified medical device.

Cardiovascular disease accounts for 30.5% of all deaths in Singapore. Yet 49% of residents did not have a health checkup in the past 12 months. BeatCheck explores whether the wearables already on people’s wrists can help close that screening gap.
